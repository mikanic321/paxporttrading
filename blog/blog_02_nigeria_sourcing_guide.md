## Introduction

Nigeria is Africa's largest economy and one of the most attractive markets for Chinese imports. With over 200 million people and a rapidly growing middle class, demand for construction materials, machinery, consumer goods, and industrial equipment continues to surge.

However, importing into Nigeria comes with unique challenges:
- **SONCAP certification** requirements
- **Complex customs procedures** at Lagos ports
- **Currency fluctuation** risks (Naira volatility)
- **Infrastructure challenges** affecting logistics

This guide provides practical, actionable advice for Nigerian importers sourcing from China.

## Chapter 2: SONCAP Certification Explained

### 2.1 What is SONCAP?

**SONCAP** (Standards Organization of Nigeria Conformity Assessment Program) is a mandatory certification scheme for all regulated products imported into Nigeria.

**Key Points:**
- Mandatory for most consumer and industrial goods
- Must be obtained BEFORE shipment
- Valid for specific product batches
- Non-compliance = goods detained at port

### 2.2 Products Requiring SONCAP

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Electrical | Cables, switches, appliances |
| Automotive | Parts, batteries, tires |
| Construction | Tiles, sanitary ware, steel |
| Chemicals | Paints, adhesives |
| Food | Processed foods, beverages |
| Textiles | Fabrics, garments |
| Toys | All toy products |

### 2.3 SONCAP Process

**Step 1: Product Testing**
- Send samples to SONCAP-accredited lab in China
- Or: Use existing test reports (if valid)
- Cost: $200-500 per product type
- Time: 5-10 working days

**Step 2: PC (Product Certificate) Application**
- Apply to SONCAP-accredited certification body
- Submit test reports and factory audit (if required)
- Cost: $500-1000 per certificate
- Valid: 1 year

**Step 3: SC (SONCAP Certificate) for Shipment**
- Apply for SC before each shipment
- Submit PC, commercial invoice, packing list
- Cost: $300-500 per shipment
- Processing: 3-5 working days

### 2.4 Accredited Certification Bodies

| Body | Website | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| SGS | sgs.com | Global, reliable |
| Bureau Veritas | bureauveritas.com | Fast processing |
| Intertek | intertek.com | Comprehensive |
| Cotecna | cotecna.com | Cost-effective |

### 2.5 Common SONCAP Mistakes

❌ **Ordering without checking SONCAP requirements**
✅ Verify certification needs before placing orders

❌ **Using non-accredited labs for testing**
✅ Only use SONCAP-recognized laboratories

❌ **Applying for SC too late**
✅ Apply at least 2 weeks before shipment

❌ **Incorrect product categorization**
✅ Work with experienced certification agent

## Chapter 3: Finding Nigerian-Friendly Suppliers

### 3.1 Supplier Characteristics for Nigeria

**Ideal Supplier Profile:**
- Export experience to Nigeria
- Familiar with SONCAP requirements
- Flexible payment terms
- Understanding of Nigerian market needs

**Red Flags:**
- No Africa export experience
- Unwilling to assist with certification
- Demands 100% advance payment
- Poor communication

### 3.2 Questions to Ask Suppliers

1. "Have you exported to Nigeria before?"
2. "Can you provide SONCAP test reports?"
3. "What's your experience with [specific product] certification?"
4. "Can you recommend a certification body?"
5. "What's your payment terms for Nigerian clients?"

### 3.3 Payment Considerations

**Common Payment Methods:**

| Method | Risk | Recommendation |
|--------|------|----------------|
| T/T 30/70 | Medium | Standard for Nigeria |
| L/C at Sight | Low | Recommended for large orders |
| D/P at Sight | Medium | Good alternative |

**Currency Tips:**
- Most suppliers accept USD
- Some accept EUR
- Very few accept Naira directly
- Consider exchange rate hedging for large orders

## Chapter 4: Shipping to Nigeria

### 4.1 Major Shipping Routes

**China to Lagos:**
- Transit time: 28-35 days
- Frequency: Weekly
- Major carriers: MSC, Maersk, COSCO, CMA CGM

**China to Port Harcourt:**
- Transit time: 30-40 days
- Less frequent than Lagos
- Higher freight costs

### 4.2 Container Optimization

**20GP Container Costs (approximate):**
- Freight: $1,500-2,500
- Local charges Lagos: $800-1,200
- Customs clearance: $500-1,000
- Delivery to warehouse: $200-500

**Tips to Reduce Costs:**
- Consolidate shipments (40HQ more cost-effective)
- Plan around peak seasons (avoid December rush)
- Use reliable freight forwarders
- Get multiple quotes

### 4.3 Lagos Port Challenges

**Known Issues:**
- Port congestion (especially Apapa)
- Documentation delays
- Multiple government agencies
- Infrastructure limitations

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Use experienced clearing agent
- Ensure complete documentation
- Budget for potential delays
- Consider alternative ports (Tin Can vs Apapa)

## Chapter 5: Customs Clearance Process

### 5.1 Required Documents

**Essential:**
- [ ] Bill of Lading (original)
- [ ] Commercial Invoice
- [ ] Packing List
- [ ] SONCAP Certificate (SC)
- [ ] Product Certificate (PC)
- [ ] Combined Certificate of Value and Origin (CCVO)
- [ ] Risk Assessment Report (RAR)

**Additional (if applicable):**
- [ ] Import License
- [ ] NAFDAC certificate (food/drugs)
- [ ] NESREA certificate (electronics)

### 5.2 Clearing Agent Selection

**What to Look For:**
- Licensed by Nigerian Customs Service
- Experience with your product category
- Good track record
- Transparent pricing
- Office at Lagos port

**Recommended Questions:**
1. "How long have you been in business?"
2. "What's your experience with [product]?"
3. "Can you provide references?"
4. "What's your total fee structure?"
5. "What's your average clearance time?"

### 5.3 Timeline Expectations

| Stage | Timeline | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Vessel arrival | Day 0 | - |
| Documentation submission | Day 1-2 | Prepare in advance |
| Customs processing | Day 3-7 | Can extend if issues |
| Physical examination | Day 5-10 | Random or targeted |
| Duty payment | Day 7-10 | Once assessed |
| Release | Day 10-14 | Best case scenario |

**Reality Check:** Budget 2-4 weeks for clearance.

## Chapter 6: Cost Calculation

### 6.1 Total Landed Cost Formula

```
Total Landed Cost = 
  Product Cost
  + Packaging
  + Local Transport (China)
  + Export Documentation
  + Ocean Freight
  + Insurance
  + SONCAP Certification
  + Destination Charges
  + Customs Duty
  + Clearing Agent Fee
```

### 6.3 Hidden Costs to Budget

- Demurrage (container storage at port)
- Detention (container late return)
- Examination fees
- Agency fees
- Unofficial charges (unfortunately common)

**Recommendation:** Budget 10-15% buffer for unexpected costs.
