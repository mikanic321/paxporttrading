The 3 Stages of Quality Control

Professional quality control happens at three critical points:

1. Pre-Production Inspection (PPI)

When: Before production begins
Purpose: Verify raw materials and factory readiness
Cost: $150-300

2. During Production Inspection (DUPRO)

When: 20-50% through production
Purpose: Catch issues early while they can be fixed
Cost: $200-400

3. Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)

When: When 80-100% of goods are ready
Purpose: Final verification before payment and shipment
Cost: $250-500

Pre-Production Inspection Checklist

☐ Raw Materials Verification

Check material specifications match approved samples
Verify material certifications (if required)
Test material durability and quality
Confirm material quantities are sufficient

☐ Production Line Setup

Verify correct machinery is in place
Check calibration of testing equipment
Confirm worker training on product specifications
Review production schedule and capacity

☐ Documentation Review

Confirm purchase order matches specifications
Review approved samples and golden samples
Check packaging specifications
Verify labeling requirements

During Production Inspection Checklist

☐ In-Process Quality Checks

Inspect semi-finished products
Check assembly quality and workmanship
Verify dimensions and tolerances
Test functionality of moving parts

☐ Production Monitoring

Monitor production speed vs. quality balance
Check worker adherence to procedures
Verify quality control station operations
Review defect rates and trends

Pre-Shipment Inspection Checklist

☐ Product Quality (AQL Sampling)

Inspect products against approved samples
Check appearance and workmanship
Verify dimensions and weight
Test functionality and performance
Check color matching and consistency

☐ Packaging & Labeling

Verify packaging materials and strength
Check labeling accuracy and placement
Confirm barcode scannability
Verify carton markings and weight
Check pallet configuration (if applicable)

☐ Documentation

Review inspection report completeness
Verify test reports and certificates
Check packing list accuracy
Confirm commercial invoice details

Understanding AQL Standards

AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) determines how many defects are acceptable in your shipment. Here's what you need to know:

AQL Level Defect Rate Best For
AQL 1.0 1% or less High-end products, safety items
AQL 1.5 1.5% or less Standard consumer goods
AQL 2.5 2.5% or less Budget products, promotional items
AQL 4.0 4% or less Low-cost commodities

Recommendation: Most businesses should use AQL 1.5 for general consumer products and AQL 1.0 for premium items or products with safety requirements.

Common Defects by Product Category

Textiles & Garments

  • Color bleeding or fading
  • Stitching defects and loose threads
  • Size inconsistencies
  • Fabric flaws and holes
  • Misaligned patterns

Electronics

  • Non-functional units
  • Battery issues
  • Charging port defects
  • Screen dead pixels
  • Overheating

Household Goods

  • Scratches and dents
  • Poor finishing
  • Assembly issues
  • Material quality problems
  • Packaging damage

When to Reject a Shipment

Don't hesitate to reject a shipment if:

  • Defect rate exceeds your AQL level
  • Safety-critical defects are found
  • Major discrepancies in quantity
  • Wrong products or specifications
  • Packaging won't protect products in transit

⚠️ Important

Never pay the balance before pre-shipment inspection is complete and satisfactory. This is your leverage to ensure the factory addresses any issues.
