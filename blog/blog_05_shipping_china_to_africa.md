## 2. Shipping Methods Compared

### 2.1 Sea Freight (Most Common)

Sea freight handles over 90% of China-Africa trade volume. It's the most cost-effective option for large shipments.

**Full Container Load (FCL):**
- Best for: Shipments filling at least 60-70% of a container
- Container sizes: 20ft (28-30 CBM), 40ft (58-60 CBM), 40ft HC (68 CBM)
- Cost advantage: Fixed price per container regardless of weight (up to limit)

**Less than Container Load (LCL):**
- Best for: Small shipments (1-15 CBM)
- Charged per CBM (cubic meter)
- Longer transit: Additional time for consolidation/deconsolidation

**Typical Sea Freight Costs (2026 Estimates):**

| Route | 20ft Container | 40ft Container | Transit Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shanghai → Lagos | $2,800 - $4,200 | $4,500 - $6,800 | 30-40 days |
| Shenzhen → Mombasa | $2,200 - $3,500 | $3,800 - $5,500 | 22-30 days |
| Guangzhou → Tema (Ghana) | $2,500 - $3,800 | $4,200 - $6,200 | 28-35 days |
| Shanghai → Durban | $2,000 - $3,200 | $3,500 - $5,200 | 25-32 days |
| Ningbo → Dar es Salaam | $2,400 - $3,600 | $4,000 - $5,800 | 20-28 days |
| Shanghai → Djibouti | $1,800 - $2,800 | $3,200 - $4,800 | 18-25 days |

*Note: Rates fluctuate seasonally. Peak season (Aug-Oct) can be 30-50% higher.*

**Major Shipping Lines on China-Africa Routes:**
- COSCO Shipping (Chinese carrier, excellent Africa coverage)
- MSC (Mediterranean Shipping Company)
- Maersk
- CMA CGM
- PIL (Pacific International Lines)
- Evergreen

### 2.2 Air Freight

Air freight is fast but expensive — typically 4-8x the cost of sea freight per kg.

**Best for:**
- Urgent orders or samples
- High-value, low-weight goods (electronics, spare parts)
- Perishable or time-sensitive items
- Shipments under 500 kg

**Typical Air Freight Costs:**

| Route | Cost per kg | Transit Time |
|---|---|---|
| China → Lagos | $4.50 - $7.00 | 3-5 days |
| China → Nairobi | $3.80 - $6.00 | 2-4 days |
| China → Johannesburg | $3.50 - $5.50 | 3-5 days |
| China → Accra | $5.00 - $7.50 | 3-6 days |

**Key Air Cargo Hubs:**
- **Guangzhou Baiyun (CAN)** — Largest air cargo hub for Africa
- **Shanghai Pudong (PVG)** — Major international cargo hub
- **Addis Ababa (ADD)** — Ethiopian Airlines cargo hub, connects all of Africa
- **Nairobi (NBO)** — East Africa distribution center

**Pro Tip:** Ethiopian Airlines Cargo offers some of the best rates for China-Africa air freight, with their Addis Ababa hub providing connections to 60+ African cities.

### 2.3 Rail Freight (Emerging Option)

China's Belt and Road Initiative has created new rail connections:

- **China → East Africa via Djibouti:** The Addis Ababa–Djibouti Railway connects to the port, with onward connections planned
- **China → East Africa via Mombasa:** Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) from Mombasa to Nairobi, extending to Uganda
- **Transit time:** 18-25 days (faster than sea, cheaper than air)
- **Best for:** Landlocked countries (Ethiopia, Uganda, Rwanda)

*Rail is still developing for Africa routes and availability varies. Consult your freight forwarder for current options.*

### 2.4 Express Courier (DHL, FedEx, UPS)

**Best for:**
- Samples and documents
- Packages under 30 kg
- Door-to-door delivery needed

**Costs:** $8-15 per kg (includes customs clearance)
**Transit:** 3-7 days

## 3. Understanding Total Shipping Costs

The quoted freight rate is just one part of your total logistics cost. Here's what to budget for:

### 3.1 Origin Charges (China Side)
- **Pickup/trucking to port:** ¥500-2,000 ($70-280)
- **Export customs clearance:** ¥300-800 ($40-110)
- **Port handling (THC):** ¥800-1,200 per container ($110-170)
- **Documentation fee:** ¥200-500 ($30-70)
- **Fumigation (if required):** ¥300-600 ($40-85)
- **Container loading supervision:** ¥500-1,000 ($70-140)

### 3.2 Ocean Freight
- Base freight rate (see tables above)
- **BAF (Bunker Adjustment Factor):** Fuel surcharge, varies quarterly
- **CAF (Currency Adjustment Factor):** Exchange rate surcharge
- **Peak Season Surcharge (PSS):** Aug-Oct, $200-500/container
- **War Risk Surcharge:** For certain routes (Gulf of Aden)

### 3.3 Destination Charges (Africa Side)
- **Port handling:** $150-400 per container
- **Customs clearance:** $200-600 (varies by country)
- **Import duties:** Varies widely by product and country
- **Demurrage/detention:** $50-200/day if you exceed free time
- **Inland trucking:** $200-2,000 depending on distance

### 3.4 Insurance
- **Marine cargo insurance:** 0.3-0.8% of cargo value
- **Recommended:** All Risk coverage (Institute Cargo Clauses A)
- **Minimum:** Always insure for CIF value + 10%

## 4. Country-Specific Customs Guide

### 4.1 Nigeria (Lagos/Apapa & Tin Can Island)

Nigeria is Africa's largest import market from China.

**Key Requirements:**
- **SONCAP certification** required for regulated products
- **Form M:** Import declaration form, obtained from your bank
- **Pre-Arrival Assessment Report (PAAR):** Required before cargo arrives
- **Import duty:** 5-35% depending on HS code
- **VAT:** 7.5%
- **CISS (Comprehensive Import Supervision Scheme):** 1% of FOB value

**Common delays:** Port congestion at Apapa can add 1-2 weeks. Consider using Tin Can Island or Onne Port as alternatives.

**Pro Tip:** Use a clearing agent with experience at Nigerian ports. The process involves multiple government agencies (NCS, SON, NAFDAC, NESREA) and can be complex.

### 4.2 Kenya (Mombasa)

**Key Requirements:**
- **IDF (Import Declaration Form):** Apply through Kenya TradeNet (KenTrade)
- **PVoC (Pre-Export Verification of Conformity):** Products inspected in China before shipping
- **KEBS standards compliance** for consumer goods
- **Import duty:** 0-25% (EAC Common External Tariff)
- **VAT:** 16%
- **IDF fee:** 3.5% of CIF value (min KES 5,000)
- **RDL (Railway Development Levy):** 2% of customs value

**Advantage:** SGR railway from Mombasa to Nairobi Inland Container Depot (ICD) — faster and cheaper than trucking.

### 4.3 Ghana (Tema Port)

**Key Requirements:**
- **IDF (Import Declaration Form):** Through Ghana Revenue Authority
- **Destination Inspection:** Conducted at Tema port
- **Import duty:** 0-20% (ECOWAS CET)
- **VAT:** 15%
- **NHIL (National Health Insurance Levy):** 2.5%
- **GETFund Levy:** 2.5%
- **CEPS Processing Fee:** 1% of CIF

**Note:** Tema Port has undergone major expansion (Tema Port Phase 3) and processing times have improved significantly.

### 4.4 South Africa (Durban)

**Key Requirements:**
- **SARS customs registration** required
- **Import permit** for certain goods (textiles, used goods)
- **NRCS (National Regulator for Compulsory Specifications)** approval for regulated products
- **Import duty:** 0-45% (wide range)
- **VAT:** 15%
- **Anti-dumping duties** on some Chinese products (steel, poultry, textiles)

**Warning:** South Africa has strict anti-dumping measures on Chinese goods in certain categories. Check before shipping.

### 4.5 Tanzania (Dar es Salaam)

**Key Requirements:**
- **TBS (Tanzania Bureau of Standards)** conformity assessment
- **PVoC required** — inspection in China before shipping
- **Import duty:** 0-25% (EAC CET)
- **VAT:** 18%
- **Railway Development Levy:** 1.5%

## 5. Shipping Timeline: What to Expect

Here's a realistic timeline for a typical sea freight shipment from China to East Africa:

| Stage | Duration | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Production | 15-30 days | Factory manufacturing your order |
| Quality inspection | 1-3 days | Pre-shipment inspection (PSI) |
| Inland transport to port | 1-3 days | Trucking from factory to port |
| Export customs & loading | 2-5 days | Documentation and container loading |
| Ocean transit | 20-35 days | Depending on destination port |
| Destination port clearance | 3-14 days | Customs inspection and clearance |
| Inland delivery | 1-7 days | Trucking to your warehouse |
| **Total** | **45-90 days** | **Order to delivery** |

**How to reduce transit time:**
1. Start customs documentation while goods are in transit
2. Use a reliable customs broker at the destination
3. Ensure all certifications (PVoC, SONCAP, etc.) are completed before shipping
4. Choose direct shipping routes (avoid transshipment when possible)
5. Pay duties promptly to avoid storage charges

## 6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Underestimating Total Costs
Many first-time importers only budget for the freight rate and are shocked by port charges, duties, and inland transport. **Always calculate total landed cost before placing an order.**

### ❌ Mistake 2: Skipping Insurance
"It won't happen to me" — until it does. Marine insurance costs less than 1% of cargo value but protects you from total loss. **Always insure your cargo.**

### ❌ Mistake 3: Wrong HS Code Classification
Using the wrong Harmonized System code can result in:
- Higher duties than necessary
- Customs holds and inspections
- Fines and penalties
- Goods being confiscated

### ❌ Mistake 4: Ignoring Packaging Requirements
Poor packaging leads to damage claims. Ensure:
- Wooden packaging has ISPM 15 treatment stamp
- Electronics have proper moisture protection
- Fragile items have adequate cushioning
- Containers are properly stuffed and braced

### ❌ Mistake 5: Not Getting PVoC/SONCAP in Advance
These pre-shipment inspections must be done in China before the goods ship. Forgetting them means your goods sit at the destination port accumulating demurrage charges while you scramble to get compliant.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does shipping from China to Africa take?
Sea freight typically takes 20-40 days depending on the destination. East Africa (Mombasa, Dar es Salaam) is 20-30 days. West Africa (Lagos, Tema) is 28-40 days. Air freight is 2-6 days.

### What's the cheapest way to ship from China to Africa?
Sea freight FCL is the most economical for large volumes. For small shipments (under 2 CBM), LCL sea freight is cheapest. Break-even point between sea and air is usually around 200-300 kg — below that, air may be more cost-effective when you factor in warehousing costs.

### Do I need an import license to import from China?
Most African countries don't require a general import license, but you need:
- Business registration in your country
- Tax registration (TIN/KRA PIN/SARS number)
- Import declaration forms
- Product-specific permits for regulated goods

### Can I ship used goods from China to Africa?
Regulations vary by country. Kenya and Tanzania restrict used electronics and clothing. Nigeria has restrictions on used vehicles. South Africa requires import permits for used goods. Check your country's specific regulations.

### What products are prohibited from importing?
Common prohibited items include: counterfeit goods, certain chemicals, weapons, expired food products, and some used items. Each country has its own restricted list — consult your customs broker.

### How do I track my shipment?
Your shipping line provides a Bill of Lading number for tracking. Most carriers offer online tracking. Your freight forwarder should provide regular updates. Paxport provides real-time tracking updates for all shipments.
