**5 Red Flags When Choosing a China Sourcing Agent 🚩**

Choosing the wrong sourcing agent in China can cost you months of delays, thousands in wasted money, and a warehouse full of products you can't sell. After years in the building materials trade, I've seen it all — and I want to help you avoid the traps.

Here are 5 red flags that should make you think twice:

### 1. They Promise Unrealistically Low Prices

If a sourcing agent quotes you prices that are 30-40% below market rate, something's off. Either the quality is compromised, or they're padding hidden fees elsewhere. A trustworthy agent will give you a realistic price breakdown — not a fantasy number to win your business.

**What to do:** Always request a detailed cost structure. Compare it with at least 2-3 other quotes. If the numbers don't add up, walk away.

### 2. No Factory Verification Process

A good agent doesn't just find you a supplier — they verify them. If your agent can't provide factory audit reports, business licenses, or even basic photos of the production facility, that's a major concern.

**What to do:** Ask for their factory vetting process upfront. Reputable agents will have a standardized checklist they follow for every new supplier.

### 3. They Avoid Talking About Quality Control

Quality control isn't glamorous, but it's everything. If your agent gets vague when you ask about QC procedures — pre-production inspections, in-line checks, pre-shipment inspections — consider it a dealbreaker.

**What to do:** Request sample QC reports from previous orders. A professional agent will have these ready and be proud to share them.

### 4. Communication Goes Dark After Payment

You've sent the deposit and suddenly your agent takes 3 days to reply? This is one of the most common complaints I hear from importers. Consistent communication isn't a bonus — it's the bare minimum.

**What to do:** Establish communication expectations before signing any agreement. Weekly updates with photos should be standard for any active order.

### 5. They Can't (or Won't) Provide References

Every experienced sourcing agent should have a portfolio of happy clients. If they dodge your request for references or case studies, ask yourself why.

**What to do:** Request at least 2-3 references from clients in your industry. A quick call with a past client can save you from a very expensive mistake.

---

**The bottom line?** Your sourcing agent is your eyes, ears, and quality gatekeeper on the ground. Choose carefully. The cheapest option is almost never the best option.
