Nigeria Import Guide: How to Source from China and Navigate SONCAP

Published: March 26, 2026 | Reading Time: 12 minutes | Target: Nigerian Importers

Nigeria is Africa's largest economy and one of the most attractive markets for Chinese imports. With over 200 million people and a rapidly growing middle class, demand for construction materials, machinery, consumer goods, and industrial equipment continues to surge.

However, importing into Nigeria comes with unique challenges:

  • SONCAP certification requirements
  • Complex customs procedures at Lagos ports
  • Currency fluctuation risks (Naira volatility)
  • Infrastructure challenges affecting logistics

This guide provides practical, actionable advice for Nigerian importers sourcing from China.

Chapter 2: SONCAP Certification Explained

2.1 What is SONCAP?

SONCAP (Standards Organization of Nigeria Conformity Assessment Program) is a mandatory certification scheme for all regulated products imported into Nigeria.

Key Points:

  • Mandatory for most consumer and industrial goods
  • Must be obtained BEFORE shipment
  • Valid for specific product batches
  • Non-compliance = goods detained at port

2.2 Products Requiring SONCAP

Category Examples
Electrical Cables, switches, appliances
Automotive Parts, batteries, tires
Construction Tiles, sanitary ware, steel
Chemicals Paints, adhesives
Textiles Fabrics, garments

2.3 SONCAP Process

Step 1: Product Testing

  • Send samples to SONCAP-accredited lab in China
  • Or: Use existing test reports (if valid)
  • Cost: $200-500 per product type
  • Time: 5-10 working days

Step 2: PC (Product Certificate) Application

  • Apply to SONCAP-accredited certification body
  • Submit test reports and factory audit (if required)
  • Cost: $500-1000 per certificate
  • Valid: 1 year

Step 3: SC (SONCAP Certificate) for Shipment

  • Apply for SC before each shipment
  • Submit PC, commercial invoice, packing list
  • Cost: $300-500 per shipment
  • Processing: 3-5 working days
⚠️ Important: Without valid SONCAP, your goods will be detained at Lagos port. Always obtain SC before shipment!

Chapter 3: Finding Reliable Suppliers

3.1 Key Manufacturing Regions

Product Category Best Region Why

Sanitary Ware Foshan, Chaozhou Complete supply chain

3.2 Supplier Verification Checklist

Before placing orders:

  • Verify business license and export license
  • Request factory audit video or visit
  • Check export experience to Nigeria
  • Verify SONCAP certification capability
  • Request 2-3 references from Nigerian clients
  • Test product samples thoroughly

Chapter 4: Shipping to Lagos

4.1 Port Options

Port Best For Considerations
Apapa (Lagos) General cargo Most congested, plan for delays
Tin Can (Lagos) Container cargo Better for containers
Port Harcourt Oil & gas equipment Less congested
Onne Industrial equipment Free trade zone benefits

4.2 Shipping Timeline

  • Production: 15-45 days (depending on product)
  • SONCAP processing: 5-10 days
  • Sea freight to Lagos: 28-35 days
  • Customs clearance: 7-14 days (varies)
  • Total: 55-100 days from order to delivery

Chapter 5: Customs Clearance Tips

5.1 Required Documentation

  • Commercial Invoice
  • Packing List
  • Bill of Lading
  • SONCAP Certificate (SC)
  • Product Certificate (PC) copy
  • Import License (if required)
  • Form M (processed through bank)
  • PAAR (Pre-Arrival Assessment Report)

5.2 Common Delays to Avoid

Common Issues:
  • Incomplete SONCAP documentation
  • Mismatched product descriptions
  • Undervaluation suspicions
  • Physical inspection requirements
  • Form M processing delays

Chapter 6: Payment & Currency

6.1 Recommended Payment Methods

Method Risk Best For

6.2 Managing Naira Volatility

  • Lock exchange rates when possible
  • Plan for 10-15% currency fluctuation buffer
  • Consider USD-denominated contracts
  • Monitor CBN exchange rate policies

Conclusion

Importing from China to Nigeria offers tremendous opportunities, but success requires understanding:

  1. SONCAP compliance - Start certification early
  2. Reliable suppliers - Verify thoroughly
  3. Documentation - Prepare complete paperwork
  4. Logistics planning - Account for port delays
  5. Currency management - Plan for Naira volatility
