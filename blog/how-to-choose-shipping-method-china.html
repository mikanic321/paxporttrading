How to Choose the Right Shipping Method

Sea vs Air vs Express - Find the best option for your business needs

Logistics March 21, 2026 12 min read

Choosing the wrong shipping method can cost you thousands of dollars or leave you without inventory when you need it. With three main options—sea freight, air freight, and express courier—how do you decide which is right for your shipment?

In this guide, we'll break down each shipping method, compare costs and transit times, and give you a decision framework to choose the best option every time.

Quick Answer: Use sea freight for large, non-urgent shipments (cheapest). Use air freight for medium-sized urgent shipments (balance of speed and cost). Use express for small, time-critical shipments (fastest but most expensive).

Shipping Methods Overview

🚢 Sea Freight (Ocean Freight)

Shipping by cargo ship in containers.

  • Transit time: 25-40 days
  • Best for: Large shipments, non-urgent goods
  • Cost: Lowest per kg/CBM

✈️ Air Freight

Shipping by cargo aircraft.

  • Transit time: 7-14 days
  • Best for: Medium shipments, time-sensitive goods
  • Cost: Moderate (5-10x sea freight)

📦 Express Courier (DHL/FedEx/UPS)

Door-to-door courier service.

  • Transit time: 3-7 days
  • Best for: Small shipments, urgent deliveries
  • Cost: Highest per kg

Decision Framework: Which Method to Choose?

🤔 Ask Yourself:

  1. How urgent is the shipment?
    • Need in 3-5 days → Express
    • Need in 7-14 days → Air Freight
    • Can wait 25-40 days → Sea Freight
  2. What's your shipment size?
    • Under 100 kg → Express or Air
    • 100-1000 kg → Air Freight
    • Over 1000 kg or 2+ CBM → Sea Freight
  3. What's your budget priority?
    • Minimize cost → Sea Freight
    • Balance cost/speed → Air Freight
    • Speed is worth extra cost → Express
  4. What type of products?
    • High-value, fragile → Air or Express
    • Bulk, non-fragile → Sea Freight
    • Time-sensitive (fashion, electronics) → Air

When to Use Each Method

✅ Use Sea Freight When:

  • Shipment is over 2 CBM or 1000 kg
  • You have 30+ days before you need the goods
  • Cost is more important than speed
  • Products are not time-sensitive
  • You're ordering inventory for future sales
  • Products are durable and not fragile

✅ Use Air Freight When:

  • You need goods in 1-2 weeks
  • Shipment is 100-1000 kg
  • Products are high-value relative to weight
  • You're restocking fast-selling items
  • Sea freight is too slow but express is too expensive

✅ Use Express When:

  • You need goods in under 1 week
  • Shipment is small (under 100 kg)
  • You're sending samples
  • Products are urgently needed
  • You want door-to-door convenience
  • You need detailed tracking

Pro Tips for Each Method

🚢 Sea Freight Tips

  • Book 2-3 weeks in advance during peak season
  • Consider FCL (full container) if shipping 10+ CBM
  • Factor in port fees and customs clearance time
  • Get cargo insurance (damage is more common)
  • Track Chinese holidays (CNY shutdown)

✈️ Air Freight Tips

  • Chargeable weight is actual weight or volumetric (whichever is higher)
  • Volumetric formula: Length × Width × Height (cm) ÷ 6000
  • Book early for space during peak times
  • Consider direct flights vs. transshipment
  • Air freight is better for dense, high-value goods

📦 Express Tips

  • Negotiate rates if shipping regularly
  • Compare DHL, FedEx, UPS for best price
  • Use your own account for better rates
  • Consider duties and taxes (DDP vs DDU)
  • Packaging matters - oversized packages cost more

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Choosing sea freight for urgent orders
  • ❌ Using express for large shipments (too expensive)
  • ❌ Not calculating volumetric weight for air
  • ❌ Forgetting about customs clearance time
  • ❌ Not comparing quotes from multiple forwarders
  • ❌ Ignoring seasonal price fluctuations
