Payment Methods for China Suppliers

T/T, L/C, Trade Assurance, PayPal. Which is safest? Which is cheapest?

Finance March 21, 2026 10 min read

Choosing the right payment method can protect you from fraud, reduce fees, and give you leverage in disputes. But with so many options—T/T, L/C, Trade Assurance, PayPal—which should you use?

Quick Answer: For most orders, use T/T (wire transfer) with 30% deposit, 70% before shipping. For first orders under $5,000, use Alibaba Trade Assurance. For large orders ($50,000+), consider Letter of Credit.

Payment Methods Compared

Method Cost Safety Speed Best For
T/T (Wire) $20-50 Medium 1-3 days Regular orders
Trade Assurance 2-3% High Instant First orders
Letter of Credit $200-500 Very High 5-10 days Large orders
PayPal 4-5% High Instant Samples

1. T/T (Telegraphic Transfer / Wire Transfer)

How It Works

Bank-to-bank electronic transfer. Most common for China trade.

Standard Terms

  • 30% deposit to start production
  • 70% balance before shipping

Pros

  • Low fees ($20-50 per transfer)
  • Fast (1-3 business days)
  • Widely accepted

Cons

  • No buyer protection
  • Irreversible once sent
  • Need to trust supplier
⚠️ Never pay 100% upfront via T/T. Always use staged payments with inspection before final payment.

2. Alibaba Trade Assurance

How It Works

Alibaba's escrow service. Payment held until you confirm receipt.

Pros

  • Buyer protection
  • Dispute resolution
  • Refund if quality issues

Cons

  • 2-3% fee
  • Only for Alibaba suppliers
  • Dispute process can be slow

3. Letter of Credit (L/C)

How It Works

Bank guarantees payment when documents are presented.

Pros

  • Very secure
  • Bank guarantees payment
  • Good for large orders

Cons

  • Expensive ($200-500)
  • Complex paperwork
  • Slow (5-10 days)

4. PayPal

How It Works

Online payment with buyer protection.

Pros

  • Buyer protection
  • Easy disputes
  • Instant

Cons

  • High fees (4-5%)
  • Many suppliers won't accept
  • Account can be frozen

Our Recommendations

  • First order under $5,000: Alibaba Trade Assurance
  • Regular orders: T/T with 30/70 terms
  • Large orders ($50k+): Letter of Credit
  • Samples: PayPal or Western Union
