1. Product Selection for Amazon FBA

1.1 What Makes a Good FBA Product?

Not every product is suitable for Amazon FBA. Here's what to look for:

  • Size & Weight: Small and light products (under 18" x 14" x 8" and 20 lbs) qualify for lower FBA fees
  • Price Point: $15-$50 retail price is the sweet spot for beginners
  • Durability: Products that don't break easily during shipping
  • Non-seasonal: Year-round demand is safer for beginners
  • Simple: Avoid electronics or complex items that require instructions

2. Finding Reliable Suppliers in China

2.1 Where to Find Suppliers

The main platforms for finding FBA suppliers:

  • Alibaba: Best for custom manufacturing and larger orders (MOQ usually 500+)
  • 1688.com: Chinese domestic platform, lower prices but requires Chinese language skills
  • Global Sources: Good for electronics and verified suppliers
  • Made-in-China: Competitive for industrial products
  • Canton Fair: In-person trade show (April & October)

2.2 Supplier Vetting Checklist

Before placing any order, verify your supplier:

  1. Business License: Request a copy and verify it's valid
  2. Factory Audit: Visit in person or hire an inspection service
  3. References: Ask for 3-5 Amazon FBA client references
  4. Experience: Look for suppliers with FBA experience (they know the requirements)
  5. Communication: Test response time and English proficiency
  6. Payment Terms: Never pay 100% upfront; use 30% deposit, 70% before shipping

3. Amazon FBA Packaging Requirements

3.1 Individual Product Packaging

Requirement Specification
Poly Bag Thickness Minimum 1.5 mil (0.038 mm)
Suffocation Warning Required for bags 5"+ opening
FNSKU Label Must be scannable, no other barcodes
Country of Origin "Made in China" must be visible
Sealed Products must be sealed in individual packaging

3.2 Master Carton Requirements

  • Weight: Maximum 50 lbs (22.7 kg) per carton
  • Dimensions: Maximum 25" x 25" x 25" (63.5 cm)
  • Label: Carton must have FBA carton label on top
  • Markings: "Made in China," weight, and "This Side Up" arrows
  • Strapping: Use strong tape (not string or wire)

4.2 Using a Freight Forwarder

For most FBA sellers, using a freight forwarder is essential. They handle:

  • Customs clearance in China and destination country
  • Consolidating shipments from multiple suppliers
  • Booking cargo space with shipping lines
  • Preparing commercial invoices and packing lists
  • Arranging delivery to Amazon warehouses

5. Quality Control for FBA Products

5.1 Inspection Types

  1. Pre-Production Inspection (PPI): Check raw materials before production starts
  2. During Production Inspection (DPI): Monitor quality mid-production
  3. Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI): Final quality check before shipping (most important for FBA)

5.2 Inspection Checklist

  • Product specifications match approved samples
  • Packaging meets Amazon requirements
  • FNSKU labels are correct and scannable
  • No defects, damage, or missing parts
  • Correct quantity per carton
  • Carton markings are accurate

Target: Minimum 30% profit margin after all costs

Formula:
Selling Price - (Product Cost + Shipping + FBA Fees + Referral Fee) = Profit
