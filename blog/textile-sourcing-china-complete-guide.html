Understanding MOQs (Minimum Order Quantities)

MOQs vary significantly based on fabric type and supplier:

Standard MOQs by Product

  • Stock fabrics: 500-1,000 meters
  • Custom dyeing: 1,000-3,000 meters per color
  • Custom printing: 2,000-5,000 meters
  • Custom weaving: 5,000-10,000 meters

Tips for Negotiating MOQs

  • Start with stock colors, then expand
  • Combine orders with other buyers
  • Offer to pay premium for smaller runs ($0.50-2/meter extra)
  • Build relationship with first larger order
  • Consider fabric agents who consolidate orders

Quality Standards & Testing

Always specify quality requirements clearly:

Essential Tests for Textiles

  • Color fastness: Washing, rubbing, light, perspiration
  • Dimensional stability: Shrinkage after washing
  • Tensile strength: Fabric durability
  • Pilling resistance: Surface fuzzing
  • Flammability: Safety standards (especially for children's wear)

International Standards

  • ISO: General quality standards
  • OEKO-TEX: Chemical safety certification
  • GOTS: Organic textile standard
  • ASTM: American testing standards
  • EN: European standards

Sustainable Sourcing Options

China is rapidly expanding sustainable textile production:

Eco-Friendly Fabric Options

  • Organic cotton: GOTS certified options available
  • Recycled polyester: RPET from plastic bottles
  • Bamboo fiber: Soft, antibacterial, sustainable
  • Hemp: Durable, low water usage
  • Tencel/Lyocell: Wood pulp based, biodegradable
  • Deadstock fabrics: Surplus from other productions

Sustainability Certifications to Request

  • GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard)
  • OEKO-TEX Standard 100
  • GRS (Global Recycled Standard)
  • Bluesign approved
  • Fair Trade certification

đźŚ± Sustainable Sourcing Tips

  • Ask for mill's environmental certifications
  • Request water usage and chemical treatment details
  • Consider digital printing (less water waste)
  • Opt for undyed/natural color fabrics
  • Plan efficient cutting to minimize waste

Pro Tips for Fashion Buyers

Before You Order

  • Always request fabric samples (A4 size minimum)
  • Test wash samples before bulk order
  • Get color lab dips approved before dyeing
  • Confirm exact fiber composition in writing
  • Check supplier's export experience to your country

During Production

  • Inspect first batch off the line
  • Monitor dye lot consistency
  • Check for defects every 1,000 meters
  • Verify packaging protects fabric quality
