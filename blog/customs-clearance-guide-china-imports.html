Customs Clearance Guide

How to import from China smoothly. Documents, duties, and avoiding delays.

Customs March 21, 2026 12 min read

Customs clearance can be the most stressful part of importing. One missing document or incorrect classification can delay your shipment for days or weeks, costing you money and missed sales opportunities.

This guide walks you through the entire customs clearance process, from arrival at the port to delivery at your door.

Key Principle: Preparation prevents delays. Having correct documentation ready before your shipment arrives can reduce clearance time from 5 days to 1 day.

The Customs Clearance Process

Step 1: Arrival at Port (Day 0)

  • Shipment arrives at destination port/airport
  • Carrier notifies you or your customs broker
  • Goods enter customs territory

Step 2: Document Submission (Day 1)

  • Submit customs entry declaration
  • Provide all required documents
  • Pay any duties and taxes

Step 3: Customs Review (Day 1-3)

  • Customs reviews documentation
  • HS code classification verified
  • Value assessment
  • Risk assessment for inspection

Step 4: Inspection (If Selected)

  • Physical inspection of goods
  • Document verification
  • Can add 2-5 days to process

Step 5: Release (Day 1-5)

  • Customs releases shipment
  • Goods available for pickup/delivery

Required Documents

  • Commercial Invoice
  • Packing List
  • Bill of Lading / Air Waybill
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Import License (if required)
  • Product Certifications

Common Causes of Delays

⚠️ Avoid These Mistakes

  • Incomplete or incorrect documentation
  • Wrong HS code classification
  • Undervalued goods
  • Missing import licenses
  • Inconsistent information across documents

Tips for Smooth Clearance

✅ Best Practices

  • Work with experienced customs broker
  • Double-check all documents before submission
  • Use correct HS codes
  • Declare accurate values
  • Respond quickly to customs inquiries
